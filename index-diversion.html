RESIDENTIAL TRASH DIVERSION

WHAT IS TRASH DIVERSION?

Trash diversion measures the percentage of total waste that is
diverted from landfills and incinerators through
recycling, composting, and reuse programs.

The City aims to increase waste diversion on a municipal-level
by reducing both residential and commerical waste generation.

However, commercial data is obtained through different channels and
may not be updated regularly.

Thus, the following data focuses on "Residential Trash Diversion"
which is a key index of tracking the City's progress in acheiving its goals.

HAS THERE BEEN AN IMPROVEMENT OVER THE YEARS?

As the City's efforts to increase waste diversion
are still in their early stages of implementation,
the Residential Trash Diversion rate has been rather stagnant over the years.

The City hopes to address this issue in the near future
by targeting specific districts/neighborhoods with low trash diversion rates.

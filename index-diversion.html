WHAT IS TRASH DIVERSION?

Trash diversion refers to the percentage of
total waste that is diverted from landfills and incinerators
through recycling, composting, and reuse programs.

CALCULATING TRASH DIVERSION RATE

Trash diversion is calculated by a simple formula of
dividing the volume of recycling and compost
over total volume of waste.

HAS THERE BEEN AN IMPROVEMENT OVER THE YEARS?

As the City is still in the early stages of adopting ZERO WASTE policies,
the Residential Trash Diversion rate has remained relatively unchanged over the years.

The City hopes to address this issue in the near future
by targeting specific districts/neighborhoods with low trash diversion rates.
